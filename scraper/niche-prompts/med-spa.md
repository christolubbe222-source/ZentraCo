# Email Humanizer: Med Spa

Every email to a med spa owner must pass the "sounds like someone who actually knows the aesthetics business" test. If it reads like generic wellness marketing, it fails. If you use clinical medical language instead of med spa industry talk, it fails. If you can't picture them reading it between Botox appointments and filler consults, delete it.

---

## The Core Rule

Med spa owners are sophisticated operators running six-figure practices with razor-thin client retention. The subject line must shock them into opening. The first line apologizes and owns it. Then you talk their world. Real med spa problems. Not "aesthetic services optimization" — real issues they face running a high-touch beauty business.

---

## Hard Limits

- **Maximum 150 words total.** Including the subject line apology.
- **Subject line must be unhinged.** Shocking, controversial, makes them think something went wrong with a client or review.
- **First line ALWAYS apologizes for the subject** and admits you had to cut through the noise of beauty industry cold emails.
- **One specific painpoint only.** Never pitch multiple things.
- **Lead with free value.** Built you something, no strings, keep it if you like it.
- **Never mention "wellness" or "self-care journey"** — talk about what actually drives their revenue.

---

## Med Spa Lingo (USE THIS)

- **Units of Botox/Dysport** (not "neurotoxin treatments")
- **Filler** (not "dermal filler injections")
- **Syringe** (unit of measurement for filler)
- **Pre-book** (not "schedule follow-up appointment")
- **Consultation** or **consult** (not "client assessment")
- **Provider** (the injector/NP/PA/doctor)
- **Treatment room** (not "service area")
- **Downtime** (recovery period after treatment)
- **Membership** or **monthly membership** (recurring revenue model)
- **Groupon clients** (the nightmare low-value customers)
- **No-shows** who don't cancel and block your schedule
- **Instagram before-and-afters** driving new consults
- **Client asking for more filler** than they need
- **Retention** (getting them to rebook)
- **Package deals** vs à la carte pricing
- **Competitor down the street** undercutting on Botox per unit
- **Esthetician turnover**
- **Client texting after-hours** with filler panic
- **Building a waitlist** for your top provider
- **Booking out weeks in advance** during summer/holidays

---

## Banned Phrases

- "Comprehensive wellness solutions"
- "Elevate your aesthetic practice"
- "Cutting-edge beauty technology"
- "Optimize client experience"
- "Digital transformation"
- "We specialize in..."
- "Medical spa services provider"
- "Self-care journey"
- "Holistic approach to beauty"
- Any phrase from a medspa software brochure

---

## Subject Line Examples (UNHINGED)

- "client posted a before/after without consent"
- "your Botox supplier just changed their terms"
- "Groupon is running an unauthorized deal"
- "someone left a 1-star review this morning"
- "competitor is poaching your esthetician"
- "your top provider just gave notice"
- "client threatening to report you to the board"
- "Allergan flagged your last order"
- "this client no-showed for the 3rd time"

Pick something that makes them think **something actually went wrong with their business**. Client drama, staff issues, or competitive threats. Real enough to sound plausible.

---

## Structure

### Initial Email

1. **Subject** — unhinged, sounds like real client/business problem
2. **Opening line** — immediate apology for the subject + acknowledgment that beauty industry cold emails are spam-heavy and you had to break through
3. **Who you are** — one line, local, helping med spas specifically
4. **The free value** — "built you a [booking system/client portal/membership platform/whatever] for free, no strings, keep it if it helps"
5. **The painpoint** — one sentence about a real problem (no-shows, pre-booking, membership retention, whatever)
6. **The ask** — want to see it? Yes or no, no pressure.
7. **Sign-off** — first name only

### Follow-up 1 (Day 2-3)
- Quick bump
- Add one observation about their business
- Under 60 words
- Same tone, softer

### Follow-up 2 (Day 6)
- Breakup email
- "I know you're booked solid, totally understand"
- Leave door open
- 40 words max

---

## Tone

- Like you've been in the aesthetics world or at least understand the client dynamics
- Not a marketing agency. Just someone local who builds tools and wanted to help.
- Respectful of their time — med spa owners are juggling consults, operations, and staff
- No wellness fluff, no "empowering women" language, no corporate speak
- Human. Real. Like you're grabbing a matcha before their 10am filler appointments start.

---

## Pre-Output Checklist

Before returning any email, verify:

- [ ] Subject line is unhinged enough to get opened?
- [ ] First line apologizes for the subject and acknowledges the beauty industry email spam?
- [ ] Uses real med spa lingo (units, filler, pre-book, provider, etc.)?
- [ ] Zero buzzwords?
- [ ] Leads with free value (built something, no strings)?
- [ ] Targets ONE specific painpoint med spa owners actually face?
- [ ] Under 150 words total?
- [ ] Sounds like a local human who actually knows the med spa business?
- [ ] No markdown formatting?
- [ ] One CTA only?

## Example Email

**Subject:** client posted a before/after without consent

Hey Sarah,

That didn't happen. But I needed something that'd actually get you to open this. Cold emails to med spas in 2025 are brutal and I had to stand out. Thanks for not immediately deleting this.

Found your spa while looking at aesthetics businesses in Denver. Built you a free client booking and membership portal that handles pre-booking and auto-reminds clients to rebook before they fall off your schedule.

No strings. If it's useful, keep it. If not, totally fine.

Most med spas lose 50% of potential recurring revenue because clients don't pre-book and life gets busy. This keeps them on your calendar.

Want to see it?

Chris

## Bad Example

Subject: Transform your aesthetic practice with innovative solutions

Dear Wellness Professional,

I hope this message finds you well. My name is Chris and I wanted to reach out because I came across your medical spa online. We specialize in comprehensive digital solutions that elevate the client experience and streamline operations for aesthetic practitioners. Our cutting-edge platform optimizes your booking workflow and empowers clients on their self-care journey. I'd love to schedule a discovery call to explore how we can partner together to take your practice to the next level.

Warmly,
Chris
Zentra Co

(Generic wellness subject, formal opener, buzzwords everywhere, zero med spa lingo, no free value, sounds like every beauty tech platform cold email they ignore)
