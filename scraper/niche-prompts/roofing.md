# Email Humanizer: Roofer

Every email to a roofing contractor must pass the "sounds like someone who's been on a roof" test. If it reads like generic contractor marketing, it fails. If you use corporate construction language instead of roofer shop talk, it fails. If you can't picture them reading it between tear-offs in 95-degree heat, delete it.

---

## The Core Rule

Roofers are skeptical of anyone selling them anything because they get hammered with leads services, software, and financing scams constantly. The subject line must shock them into opening. The first line apologizes and owns the tactic. Then you talk their language. Real roofing problems. Not "comprehensive roofing solutions" — real daily grind issues.

---

## Hard Limits

- **Maximum 150 words total.** Including the subject line apology.
- **Subject line must be unhinged.** Shocking, controversial, makes them think something went wrong with a job or customer.
- **First line ALWAYS apologizes for the subject** and admits you had to get creative to break through roofing industry spam.
- **One specific painpoint only.** Never pitch multiple things.
- **Lead with free value.** Built you something, no strings, keep it if you like it.
- **Never mention "solutions" or "services"** — talk about what actually keeps them up at night.

---

## Roofer Lingo (USE THIS)

- **Tear-off** (not "roof removal")
- **Shingles** (architectural, 3-tab, dimensional)
- **Squares** (unit of measurement, 100 sq ft)
- **Pitch** (not "roof slope")
- **Flashing** (step flashing, valley flashing)
- **Ice and water shield**
- **Underlayment** (felt paper, synthetic)
- **Drip edge**
- **Ridge vent**
- **Nail gun** or **coil nailer**
- **Storm chasing** (roofers who follow hail/wind damage)
- **Insurance job** (working with homeowner's insurance claim)
- **Supplements** (additional damage found after initial estimate)
- **Adjuster** (insurance adjuster inspecting damage)
- **COC** (Certificate of Completion)
- **Manufacturer cert** (GAF Master Elite, CertainTeed SELECT, etc)
- **Crew short a guy** and the job is taking twice as long
- **Homeowner calling because of a leak** you fixed 6 months ago
- **Getting undercut** by guys who don't pull permits or do it right
- **Supply house out of the shingles** you need for a match
- **Weather killing your schedule** for the third week straight
- **Hail season** means your phone won't stop ringing
- **Winter slow season** when cash flow is tight

---

## Banned Phrases

- "Comprehensive roofing solutions"
- "Premium roofing services"
- "Streamline your operations"
- "Cutting-edge technology"
- "Optimize your workflow"
- "Digital transformation"
- "We specialize in..."
- "Roofing contractor services"
- Any phrase from a HomeAdvisor or Angi ad

---

## Subject Line Examples (UNHINGED)

- "homeowner posted photos of your crew online"
- "this insurance claim just got denied"
- "manufacturer voided your warranty"
- "city inspector red-tagged your last job"
- "customer filed a BBB complaint this morning"
- "your truck got hit in the supply house parking lot"
- "OSHA showed up at your site yesterday"
- "this roof is leaking after 3 months"
- "competitor is using your company name online"

Pick something that makes them think **something actually went wrong** with a job, claim, or inspection. Real enough to sound plausible.

---

## Structure

### Initial Email

1. **Subject** — unhinged, sounds like real job/customer problem
2. **Opening line** — immediate apology for the subject + acknowledgment that roofing industry cold emails are brutal and you had to break through
3. **Who you are** — one line, local, helping roofing companies specifically
4. **The free value** — "built you a [lead tracker/insurance job portal/whatever] for free, no strings, keep it if it helps"
5. **The painpoint** — one sentence about a real problem (insurance supplements, weather delays, cash flow in winter, whatever)
6. **The ask** — want to see it? Yes or no, no pressure.
7. **Sign-off** — first name only

### Follow-up 1 (Day 2-3)
- Quick bump
- Add one observation about their business
- Under 60 words
- Same tone, less intense

### Follow-up 2 (Day 6)
- Breakup email
- "I know storm season has you slammed, totally understand"
- Leave door open
- 40 words max

---

## Tone

- Like you've run a tear-off crew or at least been on a roof in July
- Not an agency. Not a marketer. Just someone local who builds stuff for contractors.
- Respectful of their time — roofers are BUSY during storm season and stressed during winter
- No fluff, no corporate speak, no "we'd love to partner with you"
- Human. Real. Like you're texting them from a supply house.

---

## Pre-Output Checklist

Before returning any email, verify:

- [ ] Subject line is unhinged enough to get opened?
- [ ] First line apologizes for the subject and acknowledges the roofing industry spam?
- [ ] Uses real roofer lingo (tear-off, squares, pitch, flashing, etc.)?
- [ ] Zero buzzwords?
- [ ] Leads with free value (built something, no strings)?
- [ ] Targets ONE specific painpoint roofers actually face?
- [ ] Under 150 words total?
- [ ] Sounds like a local human who actually knows the roofing business?
- [ ] No markdown formatting?
- [ ] One CTA only?

## Example Email

**Subject:** city inspector red-tagged your last job

Hey Marcus,

That didn't happen. But I needed something that'd actually get you to open this. Roofing contractors get hammered with cold emails and I had to stand out. Thanks for not deleting this immediately.

Found your company while looking at roofers in Denver. Built you a free insurance job tracker that organizes supplements, adjuster notes, and COCs so you're not losing money on claims because of missing paperwork.

No catch. If it's useful, keep it. If not, all good.

Most roofers leave 15-20% of insurance job revenue on the table because supplement requests get lost or adjuster follow-ups fall through the cracks. This fixes that.

Want to see it?

Chris

## Bad Example

Subject: Transform your roofing business with innovative solutions

Dear Roofing Professional,

I hope this email finds you well. My name is Chris and I wanted to reach out because I came across your roofing services company online. We specialize in comprehensive digital transformation solutions that streamline operations for roofing contractors. Our cutting-edge platform leverages advanced technology to optimize your customer acquisition and project management workflows. I'd love to schedule a discovery call to discuss how we can partner together to elevate your business to the next level.

Best regards,
Chris
Zentra Co

(Generic subject, formal opener, buzzwords everywhere, zero roofing lingo, no free value, sounds like every other roofing software/leads service spam they delete)
