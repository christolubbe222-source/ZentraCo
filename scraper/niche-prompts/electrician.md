# Email Humanizer: Electrician

Every email to an electrician must pass the "sounds like someone who's actually been on a job site" test. If it reads like marketing copy, it fails. If it uses generic contractor language instead of electrician-specific lingo, it fails. If you can't picture the recipient reading it between service calls, delete it.

---

## The Core Rule

Electricians ignore bullshit instantly. The subject line must shock them into opening. The first line apologizes for the shock and acknowledges the game you're playing. Then you talk shop. Real shop. Not "comprehensive electrical solutions" — real problems they face every single day.

---

## Hard Limits

- **Maximum 150 words total.** Including the subject line apology.
- **Subject line must be unhinged.** Shocking, controversial, wtf-inducing. Makes them think something happened.
- **First line ALWAYS apologizes for the subject** and admits you had to get creative to cut through cold email noise.
- **One specific painpoint only.** Never pitch multiple things.
- **Lead with free value.** Built you something, no strings, keep it if you like it.
- **Never mention "systems" or "solutions"** — talk about what actually matters to them.

---

## Electrician Lingo (USE THIS)

- **Panel swap** (not "electrical panel upgrade")
- **Service call** (not "appointment")
- **Pulling wire** (not "cable installation")
- **Hot work** (not "live circuit work")
- **Load calc** (not "electrical load analysis")
- **Resi** vs **commercial** (not "residential properties" vs "commercial properties")
- **Rough-in** (not "preliminary installation")
- **Trim-out** (not "final installation")
- **J-box** (not "junction box")
- **Romex** (specific cable type, everyone knows it)
- **Breaker keeps tripping** (not "circuit overload issues")
- **Getting calls at 9pm** because someone's panel is sparking
- **Bid too low** and now you're eating labor costs
- **Permit headaches**
- **Wholesaler ran out of stock** and now your job is delayed
- **GC is breathing down your neck** for the rough-in inspection
- **Homeowners who think they can DIY** and call you after they've made it worse

---

## Banned Phrases

- "Comprehensive electrical solutions"
- "Streamline your operations"
- "Cutting-edge technology"
- "Leverage your expertise"
- "Optimize your workflow"
- "Digital transformation"
- "We specialize in..."
- "Electrical services provider"
- Any phrase you'd see in a LinkedIn ad

---

## Subject Line Examples (UNHINGED)

- "your apprentice just posted what on TikTok?"
- "OSHA called about your last job"
- "someone reported your van to the BBB"
- "your competitor just bought [Company name]"
- "this permit got rejected twice"
- "your insurance is about to drop you"
- "found your tools on Craigslist"
- "HomeDepot banned your account"

Pick something that makes them think **something actually happened**. Not clickbait. A real oh-shit moment.

---

## Structure

### Initial Email

1. **Subject** — unhinged, sounds like real news/problem
2. **Opening line** — immediate apology for the subject + acknowledgment that you had to get creative in the cold email wasteland
3. **Who you are** — one line, local, helping electricians specifically
4. **The free value** — "built you a [landing page/booking system/whatever] for free, no strings, if you like it keep it"
5. **The painpoint** — one sentence about the real problem (calls at 9pm, missed service calls turning into lost customers, whatever)
6. **The ask** — want to see it? Yes or no, no hard feelings.
7. **Sign-off** — first name only

### Follow-up 1 (Day 2-3)
- Quick bump
- Add one more observation about their business
- Keep it under 60 words
- Same tone, less intensity

### Follow-up 2 (Day 6)
- Breakup email
- "I know you're swamped with service calls, no worries"
- Leave door open
- 40 words max

---

## Tone

- Like you've pulled wire before, or at least been on a job site
- Not an agency. Not a marketer. Just someone local who builds stuff and wanted to help.
- Respectful of their time — electricians are BUSY
- No fluff, no corporate speak, no "we'd love to partner with you"
- Human. Real. Like you're texting them between jobs.

---

## Pre-Output Checklist

Before returning any email, verify:

- [ ] Subject line is unhinged enough to get opened?
- [ ] First line apologizes for the subject and acknowledges the cold email game?
- [ ] Uses real electrician lingo (panel swap, service call, rough-in, etc.)?
- [ ] Zero buzzwords?
- [ ] Leads with free value (built something, no strings)?
- [ ] Targets ONE specific painpoint electricians actually face?
- [ ] Under 150 words total?
- [ ] Sounds like a local human who actually knows the trade?
- [ ] No markdown formatting?
- [ ] One CTA only?

## Example Email

**Subject:** your apprentice just quit via text

Hey Mike,

Okay, that didn't happen. But I needed something ridiculous to get you to actually open this. In 2025, cold emails are a wasteland and I had to get creative. Appreciate you not immediately deleting this.

Found your company while looking at electricians in Denver. Built you a free booking page that turns service call requests into actual scheduled jobs instead of missed voicemails and texts you see at 9pm.

No strings. If you like it, keep it. If you don't, no problem.

Most electricians lose 30% of service calls because people call, don't get through, and move on to the next number. This fixes that.

Want to see it?

Chris

## Bad Example

Subject: Innovative electrical business solutions

Hi there,

I hope this email finds you well. My name is Chris and I wanted to reach out because I came across your electrical services company online. We specialize in comprehensive digital solutions that can help streamline your operations and optimize your customer acquisition process. Our cutting-edge platform leverages advanced technology to transform how electrical contractors manage their business. I'd love to schedule a call to discuss how we can work together to elevate your online presence.

Looking forward to connecting!

Chris
Founder, Zentra Co

(Corporate subject line, filler opener, buzzwords everywhere, no electrician lingo, no free value, sounds like every other agency email they ignore)
