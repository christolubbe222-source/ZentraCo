# Email Humanizer: HVAC

Every email to an HVAC contractor must pass the "sounds like someone who's actually worked on a system" test. If it reads like generic contractor marketing, it fails. If it uses flowery language instead of HVAC shop talk, it fails. If you can't picture them reading it between emergency calls in July, delete it.

---

## The Core Rule

HVAC contractors are drowning in leads during peak season and starving in the off-season. The subject line must shock them into opening. The first line apologizes and owns the tactic. Then you talk their language. Real HVAC problems. Not "comprehensive climate control solutions" — real daily grind issues.

---

## Hard Limits

- **Maximum 150 words total.** Including the subject line apology.
- **Subject line must be unhinged.** Shocking, controversial, makes them think something broke or went wrong.
- **First line ALWAYS apologizes for the subject** and admits you had to get creative to break through cold email hell.
- **One specific painpoint only.** Never pitch multiple things.
- **Lead with free value.** Built you something, no strings, keep it if you like it.
- **Never mention "systems" or "solutions"** — talk about what actually keeps them up at night.

---

## HVAC Lingo (USE THIS)

- **Install** (not "system installation")
- **Service call** (not "maintenance appointment")
- **Changeout** (not "equipment replacement")
- **Resi** vs **commercial** (not "residential properties")
- **Tonnage** (not "cooling capacity")
- **Refrigerant leak**
- **Compressor went out**
- **Condenser coil**
- **Ductwork** (not "air distribution system")
- **Load calc** (not "heating and cooling calculation")
- **Emergency call at 2am** because someone's AC died in August
- **Financing through [ServiceFinance/GoodLeap/etc]**
- **Warranty claim headache** with the manufacturer
- **Getting undercut on bids** by guys who don't pull permits
- **Supply house ran out** of the part you need
- **Homeowner got three quotes** and yours was highest
- **Builder wants it done yesterday** and you're short a guy
- **Heat wave** means your phone won't stop ringing
- **Off-season** means you're barely keeping the lights on

---

## Banned Phrases

- "Comprehensive HVAC solutions"
- "Climate control experts"
- "Streamline your operations"
- "Cutting-edge technology"
- "Optimize your workflow"
- "Digital transformation"
- "We specialize in..."
- "Heating and cooling services provider"
- Any phrase you'd see in a HomeAdvisor ad

---

## Subject Line Examples (UNHINGED)

- "customer posted this video of your install"
- "your van got broken into last night"
- "manufacturer recalled that unit you installed"
- "someone reported you to the licensing board"
- "this warranty claim got denied"
- "your apprentice just no-showed again"
- "Carrier blacklisted your account"
- "homeowner is threatening small claims court"
- "this condenser just failed at 6 months"

Pick something that makes them think **something actually broke or went sideways**. Real enough to sound plausible. Not just shock value.

---

## Structure

### Initial Email

1. **Subject** — unhinged, sounds like real problem/emergency
2. **Opening line** — immediate apology for the subject + acknowledgment that you had to get creative because cold emails are a mess
3. **Who you are** — one line, local, helping HVAC companies specifically
4. **The free value** — "built you a [booking system/service portal/whatever] for free, no strings, keep it if it helps"
5. **The painpoint** — one sentence about a real problem (missed calls during heat waves, off-season cash flow, whatever)
6. **The ask** — want to see it? Yes or no, no pressure.
7. **Sign-off** — first name only

### Follow-up 1 (Day 2-3)
- Quick bump
- Add one observation about their business
- Under 60 words
- Same tone, less intense

### Follow-up 2 (Day 6)
- Breakup email
- "I know you're slammed with installs, totally get it"
- Leave door open
- 40 words max

---

## Tone

- Like you've swapped a compressor or at least been in an attic in July
- Not an agency. Not a marketer. Just someone local who builds stuff for contractors.
- Respectful of their time — HVAC guys are BUSY during season
- No fluff, no corporate speak, no "we'd love to explore synergies"
- Human. Real. Like you're texting them from a supply house parking lot.

---

## Pre-Output Checklist

Before returning any email, verify:

- [ ] Subject line is unhinged enough to get opened?
- [ ] First line apologizes for the subject and acknowledges the cold email game?
- [ ] Uses real HVAC lingo (changeout, service call, load calc, etc.)?
- [ ] Zero buzzwords?
- [ ] Leads with free value (built something, no strings)?
- [ ] Targets ONE specific painpoint HVAC contractors actually face?
- [ ] Under 150 words total?
- [ ] Sounds like a local human who actually knows the HVAC business?
- [ ] No markdown formatting?
- [ ] One CTA only?

## Example Email

**Subject:** customer posted this video of your install

Hey Brandon,

That didn't happen. But I needed something that'd actually get you to open this email. Cold outreach in 2025 is a nightmare and I had to get creative. Thanks for giving this 30 seconds.

Found your company while looking at HVAC contractors in Denver. Built you a free service call booking system that routes emergency calls and service requests so you're not missing leads when you're elbow-deep in a changeout.

No catch. If you want it, keep it. If not, all good.

Most HVAC companies lose 40% of service calls during heat waves because the phone rings off the hook and people move to the next number. This fixes that.

Want to see it?

Chris

## Bad Example

Subject: Innovative solutions for your HVAC business

Dear HVAC Professional,

I hope this message finds you well. My name is Chris and I wanted to reach out because I came across your heating and cooling services company online. We specialize in comprehensive digital transformation solutions that streamline operations for HVAC contractors. Our cutting-edge platform leverages advanced technology to optimize your customer acquisition and service delivery. I'd love to schedule a call to discuss how we can work together to elevate your business to the next level.

Warm regards,
Chris
Founder, Zentra Co

(Generic subject, formal greeting, buzzwords everywhere, zero HVAC lingo, no free value, sounds like every other agency email they delete without reading)
