Solar leads are expensive.
Most companies waste them.

The average cost per solar lead in the United States is between $80 and $150. Most solar companies lose 60 to 70 percent of those leads because they follow up too slowly, too infrequently, or not at all. A lead that goes cold on day three was money spent with nothing returned.

The problem isn't the lead source. It's what happens after. A homeowner fills out a quote form on a Tuesday night. Your team calls back Thursday morning. By then, three competitors have already been in contact. You're not losing on price. You're losing on speed.

The solar sales cycle is long. Automation bridges the gap.

Most homeowners take 3 to 6 weeks from first inquiry to signed agreement. During that window, they're comparing companies, reading reviews, checking financing options, and waiting to see who stays in contact. The companies that win aren't always the cheapest. They're the most present.

AI-driven follow-up keeps your company front of mind throughout that cycle without your sales team having to manually chase every lead. Quote sent but no reply? It gets nudged on day two. Site survey booked but not confirmed? The system sends a reminder. Proposal viewed but not signed? A follow-up sequence kicks in automatically.

Questions solar companies actually ask.

/01How do solar companies get more leads online?
Solar companies get more leads through a fast, conversion-first website that ranks locally, Google Ads targeting homeowners in their install area, and automated follow-up that nurtures leads through the 5 to 7 touchpoints most solar sales require. The biggest lever is speed to lead. Solar leads contacted within 60 seconds convert at dramatically higher rates than those contacted hours later.
/02What makes a good solar company website?
A good solar company website loads fast on mobile, clearly shows the savings a homeowner can expect, includes financing information upfront, displays certifications and credentials, and has a frictionless way to request a quote or schedule a site survey. Trust signals like before/after photos, verified reviews, and local project examples are critical when homeowners are making a significant financial decision.
/03How can AI help a solar company convert more leads?
AI helps solar companies convert more leads by responding to every new inquiry within 60 seconds, following up with proposals that go cold, nurturing leads through the multi-week decision cycle automatically, and handling inbound calls after hours when homeowners do most of their research. Solar sales cycles are long. AI keeps your company top of mind throughout without your team manually chasing every lead.
/04How long does it take to build a solar company website?
A Zentra Co solar website goes live in approximately 14 days from the start date. The first week covers design, copy, and structure including savings calculators and financing showcases. The second week handles AI setup, CRM integration, lead capture flows, and testing before go-live.
/05Do you work with residential or commercial solar companies?
Zentra Co works with both residential and commercial solar companies. The website design, messaging, and lead qualification logic are tailored to your specific market. A residential installer targeting homeowners needs different trust signals and conversion flows than a commercial solar company pitching to business owners and property managers.
/06What is the ROI of a professional solar company website?
For most residential solar companies, a single additional install per month from improved lead capture and follow-up pays for a full Zentra Co system within the first month. The AI receptionist and automated follow-up compound this by recovering leads that would otherwise go cold during the multi-week decision cycle.
